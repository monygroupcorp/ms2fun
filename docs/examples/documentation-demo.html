Overview

MS2.FUN is a curated launchpad for derivative art and tokens aligned to established crypto communities. Artists create projects bound to alignment vaults that automatically buy and provide liquidity for the target community's token.

What is MS2.FUN?

MS2.FUN combines a curated art launchpad with a novel alignment mechanism. Every project created on the platform is bound to an alignment vault that uses project revenue to buy and provide liquidity for an established community's token.

This creates a symbiotic relationship: artists gain access to established communities and distribution channels, while those communities benefit from increased liquidity and fresh derivative work that expands their cultural footprint.

Key Features

  • Alignment Vaults: Automated mechanisms that buy and LP target community tokens
  • Multiple Token Standards: Support for ERC404, ERC1155, and ERC721
  • Bonding Curves: Fair launch mechanics for ERC404 projects
  • DAO Governance: Community-driven curation and parameter management
  • Revenue Sharing: Transparent fee distribution to vaults and protocol

Discovering Projects

The discovery page shows all active projects across different token standards. Use filters to find projects aligned to specific communities or sort by activity, volume, or creation date.

Featured projects appear at the top and rotate based on rental auctions managed by the DAO.

Minting Tokens

Each project type has a different minting mechanism:

  • ERC404 (Bonding): Buy tokens on a bonding curve. When you hold 1 full token unit (e.g., 1M tokens), you automatically receive an NFT. Sell tokens to burn your NFT and recover funds.
  • ERC1155 (Editions): Fixed-price mints for semi-fungible editions. Each edition has a limited supply and can be collected independently.
  • ERC721 (Auctions): Participate in timed auctions to win unique 1/1 NFTs.

Staking for Vault Rewards

If a project has staking enabled, you can stake your tokens to earn a share of vault LP yield. Staking works on a share-based system where earlier stakers receive proportionally more shares per token.

Vault fees accumulate from post-graduation Uniswap V4 hook taxes (for ERC404) or artist withdrawal tithes (for ERC1155). The protocol takes a 5% cut, and the remaining 95% is distributed to stakers and benefactors.

Gas Optimization Tips

Batch transactions when possible. For ERC1155, minting multiple editions in one transaction saves gas.

Monitor gas prices and consider using services like Flashbots Protect for high-demand mints.

For Creators

Comprehensive guide to launching projects on MS2.FUN, from concept to deployment.

Choosing a Project Type

Select the token standard that best fits your creative vision:

ERC404 Bonding

Hybrid tokens + NFTs. Fair launch via bonding curve. Automatic NFT minting at threshold. Best for community-driven projects with tradeable NFTs.

ERC1155 Editions

Multiple editions with limited supplies. Fixed-price mints. 20% tithe on withdrawals. Best for serialized artwork or thematic collections.

ERC721 Auctions

Unique 1/1 NFTs. Timed auction mechanism. Best for high-value individual pieces with collector competition.

Creating a Project

The creation flow guides you through these steps:

  1. Choose Factory: Select ERC404, ERC1155, or ERC721
  2. Select Vault: Choose an existing vault aligned to your target community
  3. Configure Parameters: Set supply, pricing, bonding curve parameters, etc.
  4. Upload Metadata: Provide artwork and metadata (IPFS recommended)
  5. Deploy Contract: Execute deployment transaction
  6. Configure Advanced Settings: Set V4 hook (ERC404), enable staking, manage tiers

Revenue and Fees

Fee structures vary by project type:

  • ERC404: 1% bonding fee to treasury, 2% graduation fee split between protocol and factory creator. Post-graduation V4 hook tax goes to vault.
  • ERC1155: 20% tithe on creator withdrawals goes to vault. Creators receive 80% of mint revenue.
  • ERC721: Auction settlement fee split between protocol and vault.
Creator Best Practices

Choose a vault that genuinely aligns with your work. Authentic alignment drives better community engagement.

For ERC404, set bonding maturity time strategically. Too short and you miss price discovery; too long and you lose momentum.

Engage with your collectors. Active creators see higher secondary volume and stronger community retention.

Alignment Vaults

Vaults are the core alignment mechanism. They automatically use project revenue to buy and provide liquidity for target community tokens.

How Vaults Work

When a project generates revenue (from bonding fees, edition sales, or auction settlements), a portion flows to its designated vault. The vault uses these funds to:

  1. Buy the target community's token on the open market
  2. Add the tokens to a Uniswap V4 liquidity position
  3. Generate LP fees from trading activity
  4. Distribute LP yield back to project benefactors and stakers

Vault Lifecycle

Vaults are created through DAO governance. The process:

  1. Target Approval: DAO approves a new alignment target (e.g., "Milady" or "Pudgy Penguins")
  2. Vault Factory Selection: DAO chooses a vault strategy (currently UltraAlignmentVault)
  3. Vault Deployment: Vault is deployed and bound to the approved target
  4. Project Binding: Creators select this vault when launching projects

Each target can only have one active vault to prevent fragmentation.

Benefactor System

Projects that contribute fees to a vault are called "benefactors." Each benefactor earns a proportional share of the vault's LP yield based on their total fee contributions.

The protocol takes a 5% cut of all vault yield, with a sub-cut going to factory creators. The remaining 95% is distributed to benefactors and stakers.

Vault TVL and APY

Browse active vaults on the vault explorer page to see total value locked (TVL), estimated APY, number of aligned projects, and total benefactors.

Higher TVL generally means more stable liquidity but potentially lower percentage yields. Newer vaults may offer higher APY with more volatility.

Project Types

Deep dive into each token standard and project type supported on MS2.FUN.

ERC404 (Bonding Curve Projects)

ERC404 uses the DN404 standard to create hybrid tokens that automatically mint NFTs when you hold a full unit (typically 1M tokens = 1 NFT).

Projects launch on a bonding curve with several phases:

  • Pre-open: Configuration period before minting begins
  • Bonding: Active bonding curve, price increases with supply
  • Full: Max bonding supply reached, awaiting maturity
  • Matured: Bonding period complete, ready for liquidity deployment
  • Deployed: Liquidity deployed to Uniswap V4 with custom hook

After graduation to Uniswap V4, a custom hook taxes all swaps and routes fees to the alignment vault.

Staking and Rerolls

ERC404 projects can enable staking, allowing token holders to stake for a share of vault fees. Staking uses a share-based system with early-staker advantage.

NFTs can be "rerolled" by sending them to an escrow contract and minting a new one. This allows collectors to refresh metadata or randomize traits without selling tokens.

ERC1155 (Edition Projects)

ERC1155 projects consist of multiple editions, each with:

  • Unique artwork and metadata
  • Fixed mint price (can be updated by creator)
  • Maximum supply (can be increased by creator)
  • Independent minting status (can be paused per edition)

Mint revenue accumulates in the contract. Creators can withdraw at any time, but 20% is automatically sent to the alignment vault.

ERC721 (Auction Projects)

ERC721 projects use a timed auction mechanism for 1/1 NFTs. Features include:

  • Reserve price set by creator
  • Auction duration and extension logic
  • Anti-snipe mechanisms (time extensions on late bids)
  • Settlement fee distributed between protocol and vault

  • 95% to Benefactors/Stakers: Distributed proportionally based on fee contributions and stake shares

Creator Economics

Creators retain the majority of project revenue but contribute to vault alignment:

  • ERC404: Creators don't extract revenue directly. Value comes from token/NFT ownership and trading.
  • ERC1155: Creators receive 80% of mint revenue. 20% tithe goes to vault.
  • ERC721: Creators receive auction proceeds minus settlement fees.
Fee Transparency

All fees are enforced at the contract level and fully auditable on-chain. The DAO can adjust certain parameters through governance proposals.

Contract Architecture

Technical overview of the smart contract system, deployment addresses, and architecture patterns.

System Overview

The MS2.FUN contract system follows a registry-factory-instance pattern:

  • MasterRegistryV1 (UUPS): Central registry managing targets, vaults, factories, and instances
  • Factories: Template contracts that deploy new project instances
  • Instances: Individual project contracts created by factories
  • Vaults: Alignment vault contracts that manage LP positions
  • DAO (GrandCentral + Safe): Governance layer controlling registry upgrades and parameters

Core Contracts

GrandCentral (DAO) 0x6789...abcd Governance contract

Upgradeability

MasterRegistryV1 uses the UUPS (Universal Upgradeable Proxy Standard) pattern. Only the DAO can authorize upgrades.

Factories and instances are non-upgradeable for security. New factories can be registered via DAO proposals.

Integration Guide

Step-by-step guide for developers building on top of MS2.FUN contracts.

Reading Project Data

To display project information in your application:

// Connect to factory const factory = new ethers.Contract(FACTORY_ADDRESS, FACTORY_ABI, provider); // Get all instances const instances = await factory.getAllInstances(); // For each instance, query details const instance = new ethers.Contract(instanceAddress, INSTANCE_ABI, provider); const name = await instance.name(); const vault = await instance.vault(); const bondingStatus = await instance.getBondingStatus();

Example of minting from an ERC404 bonding instance:

// Calculate amount out for ETH in const amountOut = await instance.getBuyQuote(ethers.parseEther("0.1")); // Execute buy with slippage protection const tx = await instance.buy( minAmountOut, // Minimum tokens expected [], // Tier proofs (if applicable) { value: ethers.parseEther("0.1") } ); await tx.wait();

Querying Vault Data

const vault = new ethers.Contract(VAULT_ADDRESS, VAULT_ABI, provider); // Get vault stats const tvl = await vault.getTotalValueLocked(); const benefactors = await vault.getBenefactorCount(); // Get LP position details const position = await vault.getLPPosition(); // Query benefactor share const benefactorInfo = await vault.getBenefactor(projectAddress);
Best Practices

Always query current state before executing transactions. Bonding curves and vaults are dynamic.

Use multicall patterns to batch reads and reduce RPC calls.

Cache static data (names, symbols, vault addresses) but refresh dynamic data (prices, supplies, TVL) frequently.

DAO Overview

The MS2.FUN DAO governs the protocol using a Moloch-inspired structure with Gnosis Safe execution.

Governance Structure

The DAO uses GrandCentral, a Moloch-pattern governance contract with:

  • Shares: Voting power in governance proposals
  • Loot: Economic rights to treasury without voting power
  • Proposals: Submitted by members, voted on by shareholders
  • Safe Execution: Approved proposals execute via Gnosis Safe multisig

Conductor System

The DAO can delegate specific permissions to conductors:

  • Admin Conductor: Can register factories and vaults
  • Manager Conductor: Can manage featured queue
  • Governor Conductor: Can execute certain parameter changes

Conductors enable operational flexibility without requiring full DAO votes for routine actions.

What the DAO Controls

  • Approval of new alignment targets
  • Registration of vault factories and vault instances
  • Registration of project factories (ERC404, ERC1155, ERC721)
  • MasterRegistry upgrades (UUPS pattern)
  • Protocol fee parameters
  • Treasury management
  • Featured queue pricing and parameters

Becoming a Member

Learn how to join the MS2.FUN DAO and participate in governance.

Membership Process

To become a DAO member:

  1. Submit Application: Provide your background, contribution history, and desired shares/loot amounts
  2. Sponsorship: An existing member must sponsor your application
  3. Voting Period: Members vote on your application during the proposal period
  4. Approval: If approved, you receive shares/loot and gain member status

Shares vs. Loot

Shares: Grant voting power and proportional claims on treasury. Non-transferable.

Loot: Grant proportional claims on treasury without voting power. Non-transferable but can be rage-quit.

Most contributors receive a mix of shares and loot based on their expected participation level.

Rage Quit

Members can "rage quit" at any time to burn their shares/loot in exchange for a proportional share of the treasury. This ensures members are never locked in and provides an exit mechanism.

Contribution Ideas

Active contributors who help with development, design, community management, or ecosystem growth are strong membership candidates.

Factory creators who build new project types automatically receive economic participation through the protocol's revenue share.

Proposals

How to create, vote on, and execute governance proposals.

Proposal Types

Alignment Target

Approve a new community/token as an alignment target. Requires target details and token address.

Vault Approval

Deploy and approve a new vault for an approved target. Specifies vault factory and configuration.

Factory Registration

Register a new project factory (ERC404, ERC1155, ERC721) to enable new project creation patterns.

Treasury Action

Execute arbitrary transactions from treasury. Used for investments, grants, or other treasury operations.

Parameter Change

Update protocol parameters like fee rates, featured queue pricing, or conductor permissions.

Membership

Grant shares/loot to new members or adjust existing member allocations.

Proposal Lifecycle

  1. Submission: Member submits proposal with details and execution payload
  2. Sponsorship: Another member sponsors to move to voting
  3. Voting Period: 7-day voting window where shareholders vote yes/no
  4. Grace Period: 3-day grace period for members to rage quit if they disagree
  5. Execution: If approved, proposal is queued in Gnosis Safe for execution
  6. Safe Approval: Safe signers approve and execute the transaction

Voting Power

Voting power is proportional to shares held. One share = one vote. Loot does not grant voting power.

Quorum and approval thresholds are configurable per proposal type. Most proposals require simple majority (>50%) approval.

The DAO can use treasury funds for:

FAQ

Frequently asked questions about MS2.FUN.

General Questions

What blockchains does MS2.FUN support?

Currently Ethereum mainnet. Future support for L2s is planned.

Do I need to be a DAO member to create projects?

No. Anyone can create projects without DAO membership. The DAO curates which factories and vaults are available, but project creation is permissionless within approved factories.

Can I change my project's vault after deployment?

No. Vault binding is immutable to ensure authentic alignment.

What happens if my vault's target token goes to zero?

The vault will continue to operate but LP positions will lose value. Benefactors can still claim their proportional share of whatever remains.

Technical Questions

What is the ERC404 standard?

ERC404 uses DN404, a hybrid token standard that combines fungible ERC20-like tokens with NFT ownership. Holding a full token unit (e.g., 1M tokens) automatically mints an NFT.

How do bonding curves work?

Bonding curves use mathematical formulas to set prices based on supply. As more tokens are minted, the price increases. When tokens are sold back, the price decreases. This creates continuous liquidity and price discovery.

Why use Uniswap V4 hooks?

V4 hooks allow custom logic on every swap. We use this to tax trades and route fees to alignment vaults, creating a sustainable revenue stream.

Are contracts upgradeable?

Only MasterRegistry uses UUPS upgradeability (DAO-controlled). Factories and instances are immutable.

Economic Questions

How is vault APY calculated?

APY estimates are based on recent LP fee generation extrapolated over a year. Actual yields vary with trading volume and liquidity depth.

What's the difference between benefactors and stakers?

Benefactors are projects that contribute fees to a vault. Stakers are token holders who lock tokens to earn vault yield. Both share in the 95% yield pool.

Can creators earn from secondary sales?

ERC404 and ERC1155 don't have built-in royalties on secondary. Creators can implement this at the marketplace level or through custom hooks.
